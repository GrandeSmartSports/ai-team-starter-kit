# Module 5, Lesson 1: The Soul Document

## Bonus Module — Real-World Playbook

Everything in Modules 1-4 will get your team running. Module 5 shows you what it looks like after you've been running a team for weeks — the advanced patterns that emerge from real daily use.

---

## What Is a Soul Document?

Your Source of Truth tracks what's happening now. Your memory files track what each agent knows. But neither one captures the WHY behind your project.

A Soul Document captures your project's identity — why it exists, who it's for, and what it believes. Agents read it when they need to make decisions that require understanding the bigger picture.

---

## Why You Need One

Without a Soul Document, your agents make technically correct decisions that miss the point:

- A builder creates a feature that's powerful but confusing
- A designer picks a style that's trendy but wrong for your audience
- An agent writes copy that's clever but not how you'd talk

The Soul Document prevents this by giving every agent access to your intent, not just your instructions.

---

## What Goes in a Soul Document

```markdown
# [Project Name] — Soul Document

## Why This Exists
[One paragraph about why you started this and what problem it solves]

## Who It's For
[Describe your user. Not demographics — describe their situation, frustration, and what they wish existed]

## What We Believe
- [Belief that shapes decisions]
- [Belief that shapes decisions]
- [Belief that shapes decisions]

## How We Sound
- [Tone description]
- [Words we use / words we avoid]
- [Example sentence in our voice]

## What Success Looks Like
[Not revenue targets — describe the moment when you know it's working. What does the user experience?]

## Design Principles
- [Principle: explanation]
- [Principle: explanation]
- [Principle: explanation]

## The North Star Question
Every decision gets filtered through this question:
"[Your north star question]"
```

## Real Example

Here's a condensed version from a real project:

```markdown
# CoachPilot — Soul Document

## Why This Exists
Youth coaches are drowning. They work full-time jobs, volunteer
to coach their kids' teams, and spend hours on lineup planning,
player evaluations, and parent communication. Technology should
make coaching EASIER, not harder.

## Who It's For
The volunteer youth coach. Not professional coaches with support
staff. Not parents watching from the sidelines. The person who
said "yes I'll coach" and now has 15 families depending on them.

## What We Believe
- If the coach has to spend time figuring it out, we failed
- Simple beats powerful every time
- A coach's time with their family matters more than features
- AI should do the thinking, the coach makes the final call

## How We Sound
- Like a helpful assistant coach, not a software product
- Plain English, no jargon
- Confident but not pushy
- "Here's what I'd suggest" not "You should do this"

## The North Star Question
"Does this get the coach more time with their family?"
```

When an agent reads this before building a feature, they make different decisions. They keep things simple. They don't over-engineer. They write copy that sounds human.

---

## When Agents Should Read It

Not every session — just when relevant:

- Building a new user-facing feature
- Writing copy or documentation
- Making a design decision
- Choosing between two valid approaches
- Anything that requires judgment about "what's right for us"

Add this to your workflow protocol:

```markdown
## Soul Document
Before building user-facing features, read the Soul Document
at docs/SOUL.md. Use it to guide design and copy decisions.
```

---

## Key Takeaway

The Soul Document captures what no technical specification can — the spirit of your project. It's the difference between agents that follow instructions and agents that understand intent. Write it once, reference it when decisions require judgment.
